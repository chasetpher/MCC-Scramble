Save, Load & Sync

With Supabase configured (two lines at the top of this file's script), the roster, weekly history, course setup, sponsors, and buy-in live in a shared cloud database — every device sees the same data instantly on open or Sync Now. Check-ins, the current card, and live scores stay on this device until you save the week. Without Supabase, the app runs fully offline exactly as before.

The app also auto-saves to this device as a cache/offline fallback. The season file (JSON) is your portable backup; loading one while connected replaces the shared database for everyone (you'll be asked to confirm).

Roster CSV is one name per line — handy for bulk-adding the league.

Make the Pairings

Everyone gets one partner for the whole round; no one appears on two holes. Only holes switched on in Course Setup are used for team tees. Each hole takes 2 teams; bigger groups add a 3rd team spread across as many holes as needed (max 3 per hole). An odd player plays their own ball and is placed on a 3-team hole whenever one exists — and never draws their own ball two weeks in a row. "Fresh partners" only avoids repeating last week’s pairings — after a week apart, two players can be drawn together again. "Score-balanced" pairs last week’s low rounds with high rounds (season average as fallback) so teams start out even — the bottom-half partner is drawn randomly, still steering around repeats.

Payouts

Player count comes straight from check-in — everyone marked attending is assumed to have paid (collected manually). MGA money comes off the top: $2 × players. Under 30 players pays 2 places (60/40); over 30 pays 3 places (50/30/20).

Couples Tournament — Two-Day 18-Hole Shotgun

Tap a couple's flight badge to cycle it A → B → C — flights split the field by skill and results are ranked within each flight. It's a two-day event: pick Day 1 or Day 2, draw that day's shotgun holes (two couples per hole, same flight together wherever possible, across holes 1–18; an odd couple gets their own hole as a twosome), and enter that day's scores on the sheet. Flights rank by the two-day total.

The tournament syncs to the shared database with your other settings — every device sees the same couples, holes, and scores.

Season — Standings & Weekly History

Every card you save lands here. Standings count a "win" as being on the lowest round of the week. Pairing avoidance looks only at the most recent saved week — last week’s partners are kept apart, everyone else is fair game.
