Roster & Check-In

Check-in flow: type a few letters, then tap the name (or press Enter on a single match) — they're marked attending and the box clears for the next name. Unknown names get an "Add" option and join as attending. With the box empty, tapping a chip toggles it, so tap a green name to unselect. The ✕ removes a player from the roster.

Course Setup — Holes in Play

Tap a hole number to put it in or out of play for team tees (green = in play). Tap the par label to cycle it 3 → 4 → 5. Par 3s can be used too — just switch them on. The setup is saved in your season file.

Make the Pairings

Everyone gets one partner for the whole round; no one appears on two holes. Only holes switched on in Course Setup are used for team tees. Each hole takes 2 teams; bigger groups add a 3rd team spread across as many holes as needed (max 3 per hole). An odd player plays their own ball and is placed on a 3-team hole whenever one exists. "Fresh partners" only avoids repeating last week’s pairings — after a week apart, two players can be drawn together again. "Score-balanced" pairs last week’s low rounds with high rounds (season average as fallback) so teams start out even — the bottom-half partner is drawn randomly, still steering around repeats.

Payouts

Player count comes straight from check-in — everyone marked attending is assumed to have paid (collected manually). MGA money comes off the top: $2 × players. Under 30 players pays 2 places (60/40); over 30 pays 3 places (50/30/20).

Sponsors

These appear in a sponsor strip along the bottom of the dashboard, and they're saved in your season file.

Season — Standings & Weekly History

Every card you save lands here. Standings count a "win" as being on the lowest round of the week. Pairing avoidance looks only at the most recent saved week — last week’s partners are kept apart, everyone else is fair game.

Save & Load

The app auto-saves everything on this device as you go — roster, check-ins, course setup, sponsors, weekly history, even the current card and scores — and restores it the next time you open the file in the same browser. The season file (JSON) is your backup and your way to move to another device; older history-only JSON files load fine too.

Roster CSV is one name per line — handy for bulk-adding the league.
